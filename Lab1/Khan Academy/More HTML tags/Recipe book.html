Carbonara

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Spaghetti 200g
Egg 1
Bacon 100g
Parmesan cheese 1/2 cup, grated
Garlic 2 cloves
Salt and pepper to taste On your taste

Step 1: Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.
Step 2: In a pan, cook chopped bacon until crispy. Add minced garlic towards the end of cooking.
Step 3: In a bowl, whisk together the egg, grated Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
Step 4: Toss the cooked spaghetti in the bacon-garlic mixture.
Step 5: Remove the pan from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture, ensuring the pasta is coated evenly.
Step 6: If needed, use the reserved pasta water to achieve a creamy consistency.
Step 7: Serve immediately, garnished with additional Parmesan and black pepper.

Chicken Caesar Salad

  • Time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken breast 200g, grilled and sliced
Romaine lettuce 1 head, chopped
Croutons 1 cup
Parmesan cheese 1/2 cup, grated
Caesar dressing 1/4 cup

Step 1: Grill the chicken breast until fully cooked, then slice it into thin strips.
Step 2: In a large bowl, combine chopped Romaine lettuce, croutons, and grated Parmesan.
Step 3: Add the sliced grilled chicken to the salad.
Step 4: Pour Caesar dressing over the salad and toss until everything is evenly coated.
Step 5: Serve immediately, optionally with additional Parmesan and croutons on top.

Veggie Omelette

  • Time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
Eggs 2
Tomatoes 1 medium, diced
Spinach 1 cup, chopped
Onion 1/4 cup, diced
Cheese 1/4 cup, grated
Salt and pepper to taste On your taste

Step 1: In a bowl, whisk the eggs and season with salt and pepper.
Step 2: Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a bit of oil or butter.
Step 3: Saut√© diced onions until translucent, then add chopped spinach and diced tomatoes. Cook until the spinach wilts.
Step 4: Pour the whisked eggs over the veggies in the pan.
Step 5: Sprinkle grated cheese evenly over the eggs.
Step 6: Allow the omelette to cook until the edges set, then gently lift and let any uncooked eggs flow underneath.
Step 7: Once the omelette is mostly set but slightly runny on top, fold it in half with a spatula.
Step 8: Cook for another minute until the cheese is melted, and the omelette is cooked through.
Step 9: Slide the omelette onto a plate and serve hot.
